Helpful links for now and the future#

For finding experts to help you right now#

  • Image.sc forum 1 - a centralized forum for >60 open source image analysis tools. Posts from beginners very welcome!

Organizations that want to help#

  • AI4Life

  • The Global Bioimage Analysts’ Society (GloBIAS)

    • Find your local bioimage analyst in GloBIAS’ database

  • The Center for Open Bioimage Analysis (COBA) offers free (though limited) one-on-one consultations with image analysts (among other resources!). You can sign up for their Office Hours.

Places to learn more (or to learn where to learn more)#

  • The BioImaging Guide 2

  • Introduction to Bioimage Analysis (Pete Bankhead)

  • HMS CITE ‘favorite references’

  • Community-developed checklists for publishing images and image analyses 3

  • The Images to Knowledge conference

  • Metrics Reloaded 4,5,6 - an interactive tool for picking the right metric for measuring the accuracy of your imaging-related algorithm (segmentation, classification, etc)

  • Believing is seeing - the deceptive influence of bias in quantitative microscopy 7 - why even quantification is sometimes not enough to make sure your results are unbiased

  • The crucial role of bioimage analysts in scientific research and publication 8 - why bioimage analysis is so important, and how to work with (and support!) your friendly local bioimage analyst.

  • Enabling global image data sharing in the life sciences 9 - why and how should we share our image data when we’re ready to publish it?

  • This awesome online Nobias Advanced Scripting in Fiji Course The course assumes familiarity with Fiji but no coding experience!
